This is a journal about edible plants.

I hope it helps readers cultivate a richer understanding of our surroundings, and avoid sad grocery spinach.

How to use this map


A lot of medicinal/edible plant knowledge resides within the craniums of old folks. This map is not a replacement for for talking to wise elders.

But in my experience, those folks can be hard to find (they tend to live in distant suburbs).

I've often thought, "I wish there were a guide to the plants in my neighborhood, something I could consult without having to metro two hours."

This guide is built for that purpose: to offer people reference to a handful of plants in our neighborhood.

If you find your own favorites, please don't hesitate to add them, or shoot me a note at edwardpierrerodrigue [at] gmail

Happy ambling,

Pete Rodrigue
